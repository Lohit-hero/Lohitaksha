Master’s Project — IInd Phase: Plasma & Spatially Confined Hydrogen Atom — Comparison Theorem & Statistical Complexity Guide: Prof. Kalidas Sen, School of Chemistry, University of Hyderabad • Dec 2024 – Mar 2025 • Duration: 4 months
▶

This second phase focuses on the theoretical and computational investigation of the electronic properties of confined hydrogen atoms subjected to screened Coulomb potentials. The core objective is to analyze the interplay between quantum mechanical confinement and environmental screening effects, elucidating their influence on the system’s electronic structure and information-theoretic measures.

  1. Electronic Structure Analysis
    • Computed energy levels of the hydrogen atom under spatial confinement with screened Coulomb potentials [Hulthén / Yukawa / Hulthén(2ℓ) / Exponential-Cosine Screened Coulomb Potential].
    • Systematically investigated the combined influence of confinement and varying screening parameters on electronic properties.
  2. Information-Theoretic Measures
    • Shannon entropy to quantify uncertainty in the electron’s spatial distribution.
    • Disequilibrium to assess deviation from a uniform distribution (degree of order).
    • Statistical complexity computed by combining Shannon entropy and disequilibrium to capture the interplay of order and randomness and the system’s structural organization.
  3. Data Analysis and Interpretation
    • Extensive data analysis: visualization, statistical treatment, and interpretation grounded in quantum mechanical principles.
    • Analyzed the impact of screening parameters on statistical complexity and related observables.
