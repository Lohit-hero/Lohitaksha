Master‚Äôs Project: IInd Phase: Plasma and Spatially Confined Hydrogen Atom

**Guide: Prof. Kalidas Sen, School of Chemistry, University of Hyderabad (December 2024 ‚Äì March 2025)**

This second phase of the Master's project focused on the theoretical and computational investigation of the electronic properties of confined hydrogen atoms subjected to screened Coulomb potentials. The core objective was to analyze the interplay between quantum mechanical confinement and environmental screening effects, elucidating their influence on the system's electronic structure and information-theoretic measures.

  • **Electronic Structure Analysis:**
    • Conducted detailed calculations of the energy levels of the hydrogen atom under spatial confinement and in the presence of screened Coulomb potentials.
    • Systematically investigated the combined influence of confinement and varying screening parameters on the electronic properties.
  • **Information-Theoretic Measures:**
    • Computed Shannon entropy to quantify the degree of uncertainty or randomness in the electron's spatial distribution.
    • Calculated Disequilibrium to assess the deviation from a uniform probability distribution, providing insights into the system's degree of order.
    • Calculated statistical complexity by combining Shannon entropy and disequilibrium values, quantifying the structural organization and pattern formation within the confined hydrogen atom.
  • **Data Analysis and Interpretation:**
    • Extensive data analysis was conducted to extract meaningful insights from the computational results, including visualizing data, performing statistical analysis, and interpreting results in the context of quantum mechanical principles.
    • Analyzed and interpreted the impact of the screening parameters on the statistical complexity of the system.
