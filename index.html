Projects

M.Tech Project — Fe-30Mn-8Al-1C Steel & Hydrogen Embrittlement (IIT Kanpur, Dec 2025–Present)

Investigating the mechanical and microstructural evolution of Fe-30Mn-8Al-1C steel under hydrogen. The work integrates fracture toughness tests, controlled hydrogen charging, and microanalysis (XRF/SEM/TEM) with computational modeling to uncover failure modes and propose mitigation strategies for hydrogen-assisted cracking.

  • Design of test matrices for varied charging conditions and crack growth regimes.
  • Microstructural quantification and defect mapping correlated to fracture behavior.
  • Data pipelines in Python for curve fitting, image analysis, and report generation.
Hydrogen Embrittlement Fracture Mechanics XRF/SEM/TEM Python

Master’s Project (Phase II) — Plasma & Spatially Confined Hydrogen Atom (UoH, Dec 2024–Mar 2025)

Applied Hulthén, Yukawa, and Exponential-Cosine screened potentials to a confined H-atom, computing energy spectra and information-theoretic measures (Shannon entropy, disequilibrium, statistical complexity). Explored how confinement and screening parameters co-shape the electronic structure and complexity.

  • Python pipelines for simulations and visualization; manuscript prepared.
  • Systematic parameter sweeps; insights into order–randomness interplay.
Screened Coulomb Potentials Information Theory Numerical Methods

Master’s Project (Phase I) — Screened Coulomb Potentials & Confined Hydrogen (UoH, Aug–Dec 2024)

Conducted an extensive literature and theoretical survey on screened Coulomb models and analyzed their impact on eigenvalues and wavefunctions for hydrogen-like systems under confinement; benchmarked results against analytical limits and explored reactivity implications.

  • Compared potential families (Yukawa, Hulthén, variants) across confinement regimes.
  • Mapped trends in level shifts and wavefunction localization.
Quantum Chemistry Modeling Benchmarking
